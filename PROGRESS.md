### 2026-07-17 — Traffic realism T1: lanes, headway, signals, car identity

User request: cars must not drive through each other, must follow traffic laws, must keep right, must take up real space, must not all look alike, and must have a purpose; pedestrians likewise; think deeper and write a design doc.

Design first: `docs/design/simulation-realism.md` defines the realism principles (readable cause-and-effect, deterministic derived state over stored state, micro rules under the macro congestion layer), inventories what already existed (every agent already has a real errand — purpose needed surfacing, not inventing), and phases the work (T1 now; junction discipline/parking/vehicle classes in T2; freight/emergency/schedules in T3). `game-design.md`'s Traffic section now points at it and its vehicle bullet describes the new movement law; `AGENTS.md` lists the doc.

Sim (worker-side, deterministic, no protocol or save-format changes): `vehicleSystem` now groups cars into lanes — one traversal direction of one edge — processed in sorted-key order, leader first. Desired advance keeps the bucket speed law, then clamps forward-only: a follower may never sit closer than `VEHICLE_HEADWAY_CELLS` (0.6) behind its post-move leader, a car whose next lane's entry stretch (base speed + headway, pre-move tail positions plus same-tick entrants) is occupied holds at the edge boundary, a car approaching a ≥3-way junction on red holds at a stop line `VEHICLE_STOP_LINE_CELLS` (0.5) out unless it was already past the line, and trip spawns defer while the curb entry stretch is occupied (`spawnBlocked` in trips). Final-leg arrivals ignore signals — the car leaves the road. Signals are stateless: `signalPhase(tick, nodeCell)` in `src/protocol/signal-phase.ts` (green 48 ticks per axis, 8-tick all-red clearance, Knuth-hash per-node offset) — pure integer math shared verbatim by sim and renderer, so no signal state crosses the worker boundary and the two can never disagree. `pedestrianSystem` applies the same lane discipline to walkers on a shared directed segment with `PEDESTRIAN_MIN_GAP_CELLS` (0.25), using global path progress so the clamp works across unit segments.

Renderer: cars offset onto the right-hand lane of their travel direction (`VEHICLE_LANE_OFFSET` 0.18, perpendicular-right in `samplePolyline`), so opposing flows occupy separate carriageways and can no longer visually intersect. Car paint and body proportions are now identity: `vehicle-style.ts` hashes (id, generation) into a 10-color everyday palette plus width/height/length variants, mirroring the pedestrian variety system; the congestion-bucket car tint is gone (`VEHICLE_BUCKET_COLORS` removed — load lives in the traffic overlay only) and per-instance scale rides the existing matrix. Traffic-light lenses left the static merged streetscape mesh: `road-streetscape.ts` now bakes only poles + housings and emits `SignalLensDescriptor`s, which `SignalLensesView` renders as an unlit InstancedMesh recolored from the same `signalPhase` the sim obeys — a red queue on screen is guaranteed to be a red light in the sim. Lens transforms rebuild only with the road network; colors only when a junction's phase changes.

### 2026-07-01 — LLM playtest → find → improve loop (5 rounds)

Played five full onboarding-to-healthy-city sessions via the automation hooks, fixing between rounds. Round 1 (naive advisor-follower): mass abandonment during onboarding (utility grace too short), ghost-town death spiral (vacancy suppressed R demand below the move-in gate forever), and a hard bug — roads could not cross power lines (ghost claimed valid, sim rejected, districts unconnectable). Round 2: bulldoze-then-build race (growth every 4 ticks rebuilt cleared cells before the road landed). Round 3: green service ghosts silently rejected (missing road/water adjacency in client validity) with useless 'Validation failed' toasts. Harness: monolithic worker advance starved command processing (now batched, 100 ticks). Fixes: grace 30→75 evals, recovery 5→3, bridge radius 2→3, vacancy penalty capped at 16 + move-in trickle above r > -0.3, road/line crossing symmetry, rubble (200-tick regrowth block), honest footprint ghosts + reason toasts. Round 5 result: one deliberate build pass → 228 population, both services placed, 74 employed, 9 commuting vehicles, zero disconnected trips, zero abandonment, treasury rising across budget cycles, "city is healthy" advisory, exact save/load restore. 61 tests green.

Known follow-ups (acceptable for now): lake-heavy terrain limits big-city sprawl (bridges are the natural next feature); level-ups are slow to witness in short sessions (no "leveled up" moment); employment is distance-based so unreachable jobs surface only via the disconnected-trips advisory.
