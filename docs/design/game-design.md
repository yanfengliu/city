# Game Design

Gameplay numeric values live in `src/sim/constants/`; presentation-only tuning values live in `src/rendering/constants.ts`. Playtesting adjusts constants, not this doc, unless a mechanic changes shape. RNG: every stochastic choice uses `world.random()`. All cadenced systems declare `interval`/`intervalOffset` as listed to stagger load. `intervalOffset` MUST be < `interval` (the engine throws otherwise), and offsets stagger modulo the system's own interval — perfect global staggering is impossible (growth's interval 4 occupies residues 1 and 5 mod 8), so the goal is that HEAVY systems avoid each other; cheap ones may coincide. Authoritative cadences: growth 4/1; move-in 8/3; trips 8/2 (phase 3); employment 8/4 (phase 3); pollution 8/0; noise 8/6; level 16/5; landValue 16/12; demand 32/14; power flood-fill 8/7 (phase 5); water flood-fill 8/1 (phase 5); congestion epoch 64/12 (phase 3); budget 1024/1023 (offset 1023 puts the FIRST budget at tick 1024 instead of charging upkeep on tick 1).

## Map and time

- Fixed grid 128x128 cells. 1 cell ≈ 8 m; 1 cell = 1 render unit. Sim runs at 20 TPS; speeds pause/1x/2x/4x (worker maps 1x/2x/4x to engine setSpeed; pause to engine pause/resume; renderer unaffected).
- Terrain: seeded simplex elevation in [0,1]; water where elevation < 0.35. Every non-water cell remains mechanically buildable land. The renderer maps land elevation to gentle 0.00–0.90-world-unit relief with a coast-flattening smoothstep curve; elevation adds no slope limits, grading costs, terrain editing, path costs, or simulation effects. Water remains one mechanically and geometrically flat plane at y=-0.12, while its seeded depth (`seaLevel - elevation`) drives a continuous renderer-only aqua-bank → clear-blue → deep-blue color ramp; shared water corners average their neighboring depths and land-touching corners stay shallow, so the tint reads as bathymetry rather than grid noise. Two renderer-only ripple bands travel with a fixed prevailing wind and perturb only the standard material's lighting normals, producing moving surface waves without changing water vertices, picking, shore seams, shadows, bridges, or simulation state; presentation wind continues while gameplay is paused and its seamless phase is not saved. Shore skirts descend from the local raised land edge to water, and sandy shoreline strips follow the raised surface only where land directly borders real water. Multi-cell stamps sit on level visual foundations without changing their integer-grid simulation footprints. Tree mask: land cells where a second noise octave > 0.62 get decorative trees; any occupancy-claiming placement clears trees on its footprint. Map generation guarantees a water body ≥ 60 cells. Decorative trees deterministically mix pointed conifers, rounded broadleaf crowns, and tall columnar forms across four coordinated green/olive/blue-green foliage families, with varied height, width, rotation, and slight within-cell placement offsets; occupancy never rerolls a tree's visual identity.
- Day = 4096 ticks for the day-counter (game time). The day/night lighting cycle runs off game time: the frame loop drives `scene.setDayFraction((tick/4096 + DAY_START_FRACTION) % 1)` and ramps the buildings' warm window-glow with `setNightGlow(1 - daylight)`. Two fixes make it playable (it had been disabled for failing both): `DAY_START_FRACTION` (0.4) phase-offsets the render fraction so a fresh game (tick 0) boots in bright late morning rather than at midnight; and night keeps a high strategy-game lighting floor (bright hemisphere fill + moonlight) so — paired with the lit buildings — it reads as a clear blue dusk, not a black-out. The render offset is purely visual: the "Day N" counter still uses the raw `tick`. Budget interval = 1024 ticks, independent of day length (income and upkeep are defined per budget interval; the budget system runs at interval 1024, offset 1023 — the first budget fires at tick 1024, not tick 1).

## Roads

- One road type in v1: two-lane road. Cost 10/cell on land; a road cell on water builds as a **bridge** at `BRIDGE_COST_PER_CELL` = 40 (4x). Upkeep per budget interval: `ROAD_UPKEEP_PER_CELL` per land cell, `BRIDGE_UPKEEP_PER_CELL` = 0.4 per bridge cell (see Economy for the authoritative values). Placement: click-drag produces an L-shaped cell path (dominant axis first); each cell becomes a road cell; mixed land/water drags charge per cell. Validators: cells in unlocked area (v1: whole map); treasury covers total cost. A growable R/C/I building in the way is bulldozed in full and paved over, exactly as a special building does (residents evicted, workers unassigned, normal rubble cooldown, no demolition fee — the cell costs the same as empty ground); a player-placed service, plant or pump still blocks and must be removed by hand. Power lines never own a cell, so one under a new road survives and keeps conducting. Bulldozing refunds 25% of the removed cells' summed actual cost, floored once per command (bridge cells contribute the premium). Rendering: land roads, pale curbside sidewalks, and vehicles visually follow the shared terrain surface while remaining ordinary flat-cost graph cells; road detail and lane markings use the same vertices. Land T/four-way junctions add compact paired traffic-light fixtures with complementary red/green faces. The fixtures are static presentation in this increment: vehicle yielding and timed signal phases require a later lane/stop-line/intersection-occupancy model.
- **Outside highway connection**: the city's fixed link to the world — a straight highway stub seeded at construction from the north edge at the horizontal center, `HIGHWAY_LENGTH` (10) cells inward (constants in `src/sim/constants/highway.ts`; gated behind the `highwayEnabled` config flag, on in the shipping worker). It is real road cells (part of the graph, so the player's network connects to it) but bulldoze-protected — `removeRoadCells` skips `HIGHWAY_CELL_SET`, so the connection can never be severed. Construction clears any water/trees under its footprint so it always lands on solid ground. It is serialized as ordinary road entities (restored on load, re-seeded on New City). Rendered distinctly by `HighwayView` (static): near-black asphalt with a dashed amber center line, fanning into a wider ramp beyond the map edge so it reads as arriving from outside. The founding advisory tip is to link a road to it. (Scope: road link only — no external-commuter economy yet.)
- Bridges are ordinary road cells to the graph, traffic, zone proximity, and save/load — bridge-ness derives from terrain (`water[i] === 1 && roadCells.has(i)`), never stored. Zoning, building growth, services, plants, pumps, and power lines reject water; underground pipes may cross it, so one pump network can serve districts across a lake. Rendering: causeway-style concrete decks stay water-relative, with railings on cell edges without a road neighbor, pylons down into the water, and coast-flattened approaches; elevation does not affect bridge classification or cost.
- Road graph (game-owned, not engine): nodes at road cells whose road-neighbor count ≠ 2 or whose 2 neighbors are non-collinear (corners); edges are maximal straight runs between nodes, weight = cell count. Edge identity is a geometry key (string of the two endpoint cells + the first path cell), so edges whose geometry survives a rebuild keep their identity. Full rebuild on any road change; `topologyVersion++`; path cache cleared; in-flight vehicles remap via the geometry-key map (see Traffic). Cells store their containing edge id for O(1) access-node lookup.
- Edge capacity = 6 vehicles per cell of length. Congestion c = vehiclesOnEdge / capacity, quantized to buckets 0–3 at thresholds 0.4 / 0.75 / 1.0.

## Zoning and building growth

- Zones: residential (R), commercial (C), industrial (I), low density. Zone tool paints unzoned land cells within Chebyshev distance 2 of a road cell (this matches the maximum growth reach of a 2x2 footprint anchored on a road-adjacent cell — distance-3 cells could never develop); painting never replaces an existing zone, which must be erased with the dezone tool first. Mixed drags preserve existing zones while filling eligible empty cells. Zoning is free; zoned cells render as colored grid tint (R green, C blue, I yellow-orange) with small, low-opacity inset planning parcels on visible, undeveloped cells so empty zones read as a modern planning overlay rather than beige construction pads.
- Growth system (interval 4, offset 1): while zone demand > 0, up to 4 growth attempts per run per zone type. An attempt picks a random zoned, empty, road-adjacent (4-dir) cell; tries a 2x2 footprint of same-zone empty cells first, else 1x1; requires footprint free in OccupancyGrid. Spawns a level-1 building entity with the footprint blocked (v1 buildings are unrotated boxes). Rendering keeps those simple footprints but gives every growable literal windows on all four walls and a physically distinct zone frontage: pastel-sage residential has pitched roofs, chimneys, a front door, stoop, and porch canopy; clear-blue commercial has denser glazing, a double entry, awning, sign band, blade sign, and taller rooftop core; warm-amber industrial has sparse high clerestories, a loading bay, personnel door, dock, hood, bollards, and broad low HVAC. Entrances stay ground-floor sized as bodies level up. Building bodies and frontage pieces are non-emissive so daylight preserves their hue; only live window panels begin a modest warm glow after dusk. Abandoned buildings use a lighter neutral-gray treatment, retain their dark frontage, and hide all glowing window geometry.
- Capacity per building = perCellBase(zone, level) x footprintCells, measured in citizen units (see Demand). Residents (R): L1 1, L2 2, L3 3 per cell. Job slots: C L1 1, L2 2, L3 3; I L1 1, L2 2, L3 2 per cell.
- Desirability score per building, evaluated by the level system (interval 16, offset 5), is zone-aware. R and C: `score = 0.5 x landValue(cell) + 8 x serviceCoverageCount(0–5) + 10 x (powered && watered) - taxPenalty`, where taxPenalty = 2 x max(0, taxRate(zone) - 9). Industrial: `score = 0.1 x landValue(cell) + 8 x serviceCoverageCount + (powered && watered ? 10 : 0) + 15` (industrial base) — the low land-value weight plus the base keeps industrial clusters from abandoning over their own pollution. With neutral inputs (landValue 30, no coverage, utilities satisfied) the R/C score is 25 — intentionally stable at level 1, below the level-up thresholds. Level-up to 2 at score ≥ 45 for 3 consecutive evaluations; to 3 at score ≥ 70 for 3 consecutive evaluations AND education coverage. There is no level-down in v1 — decline is expressed through abandonment. Abandonment has two independent paths: (a) bad location — score < 12 for 10 consecutive evaluations, but ONLY while utilities are satisfied (a merely-unpowered building is never abandoned on this fast ~8s path); and (b) missing utilities — unpowered-or-unwatered for 75 consecutive evaluations (~60s at 1x) regardless of score. Both streaks are strictly CONSECUTIVE: returning to healthy resets them, so restoring power/water clears the utility streak and intermittent power — e.g. brownout flicker on an undersized plant — grants a fresh 60s grace each time rather than accumulating toward abandonment (playtest round 9). Missing power/water strips the +10 utility bonus from the score, so without path (a)'s utility gate a fresh, pollution-adjacent district mass-abandons in ~8s before the player can wire networks — the gate keeps the long utility grace authoritative during onboarding (playtest round 8). Abandoned = no taxes, evicts residents/jobs, grey render, excluded from all demand/capacity aggregates, emits no pollution or noise. Recovery: 3 consecutive healthy evaluations un-abandon at level 1, where "healthy" is the exact complement of abandonment: score ≥ 12 AND powered AND watered. Bulldoze always allowed (no refund for grown buildings).
- Industrial buildings emit pollution; commercial emit noise (see Fields). Abandoned buildings emit neither.

## Demand (RCI)

Recomputed every 32 ticks (interval 32, offset 14). ALL sim math uses citizen entities (households) as the canonical unit; population = 3 x citizens applies only in UI display. Abandoned buildings are excluded from ALL aggregates (jobs, capacity, slots). Let P = citizen count, jobsTotal/jobsFilled, U = unemployed citizens, V = free housing capacity in citizen units.

- R = clamp[-1,1]( (0.8 x (jobsTotal - jobsFilled) + 4 - 0.5 x V) / 16 ) - taxDemandPenalty(R)
- C = clamp[-1,1]( (0.30 x P - commercialJobSlots) / 10 ) - taxDemandPenalty(C)
- I = clamp[-1,1]( (1.2 x U + 0.09 x P - 0.4 x industrialJobSlots) / 13 ) - taxDemandPenalty(I)
- taxDemandPenalty(zone) = 0.05 x max(0, taxRate(zone) - 9). Growth requires demand > 0 for that zone.

## Citizens and employment

- One citizen entity = one household of exactly 3 people (population display = 3 x citizens). The hot citizen component retains home, one work slot, logical activity phase, next activity, current traveller member, and an optional generation-guarded outing assignment. Cold profile/history components retain a deterministic household name; three members with stable ids, given names, ages, life stages, education, and roles; and the newest 8 meaningful biography events. A person is identified as `(citizen id, citizen generation, member id)`. A visible walker is one named household member performing that household's current activity, not decorative population and not three extra citizen entities. The honest current limit is one employment slot, one household happiness score, one activity plan, and at most one active walker or car per household; target scale remains ≤ 2,000 household entities (6,000 named people).
- Profile generation is a pure hash of city seed, household id/generation, and home, consuming no simulation RNG. The primary worker's role tracks unemployment or its C/I workplace; work selects that member, shopping selects another adult when present, and rest selects a senior when present. Leisure is venue-specific: a household containing a child or teen prefers a park and sends its youngest member; otherwise it prefers a community garden and sends a senior when present or its oldest adult. A missing or unreachable preferred venue falls through to the other green venue, then shops. Move-in, hire, job loss, outing departure, and stranding append deterministic tick-stamped events; “outing departure” means the person left, not that the outing completed. Old snapshots without profile/history components derive stable fallback names and current employment roles without inventing prior events, expose the fallback provenance explicitly, and assign an in-flight path without a stored member its valid household traveller or deterministic primary member.
- Move-in system (interval 8, offset 3): if R demand > 0 and vacancies exist, spawn `min(freeHousingCapacity, 1 + floor(Rdemand x 5))` citizens into residential buildings with space (deterministic pick via world.random), where freeHousingCapacity = Σ over non-abandoned R buildings of (capacity - residents), in citizen entities. Move-out: abandoned/bulldozed homes evict; evicted citizens leave the city (despawned) if no vacancy found within one evaluation.
- Employment system (interval 8, offset 4; phase 3): assigns unemployed citizens to buildings with free job slots, nearest-first among REACHABLE workplaces — the workplace's road-graph access node must share a connected component (`RoadGraph.cellComponent`, recomputed on every graph rebuild) with the home's access node, so citizens never take jobs their commute cannot route to. Homes or workplaces without road access are skipped that run. Up to 32 assignments per run. Bulldozed/abandoned workplaces unassign their workers; severing the only route AFTER assignment leaves workers employed but their trips cancel as disconnected (the advisory path).

## Traffic

Micro-level behavior (right-hand lanes, car-following headway, junction signals, agent identity) is specified in `simulation-realism.md`; this section holds the macro trip/congestion model and its tuned values.

- Trip system (interval 8, offset 2): each run, up to 24 employed households without an active trip are considered through a deterministic rotating cursor. Their cycle is home → work → home → free time → home, where the free-time slot is a weighted draw between shopping, a leisure outing, and resting at home. A leisure outing tries the profile-preferred kind among the nearest six reachable parks within 32 road cells or community gardens within 16, falls through to the other green kind, then one of the nearest six shops. Happiness raises leisure weight, unhappiness and recent stranding raise rest weight, each child/teen adds 0.75 leisure weight, and each senior adds 0.75 rest weight, so stored household composition changes behavior rather than serving only as panel text. Work may be at a commercial or industrial building; shopping requires the nearest road-reachable commercial building that is live, staffed, powered, and watered. Work routes of at most 24 road cells walk unless they cross a bridge; longer/bridge commutes retain vehicles. Shopping and leisure always walk; parks and gardens never book a retail sale; rest spawns no agent. Global caps are 600 vehicles and 256 pedestrians; requests wait when their mode is full. Unroutable trips cancel, back off 128 ticks, increment `disconnectedTrips`, and append a stranding event for the current traveller.
- Pathfinding: `findPath` over the road graph (nodes/edges), batched via a `PathRequestQueue` budgeted at 8 resolutions/tick. PathCache key is (fromNode, toNode) only; a single monotonic `pathVersion` counter bumps on EITHER topology change or congestion-epoch change (no packed-version arithmetic), invalidating stale entries; `clearCache()` on topology change. Edge cost = length x (1 + 0.5 x congestionBucket).
- Pedestrian pathfinding uses the actual first road cell adjacent to each building footprint, not a compressed graph endpoint. A separate topology-versioned road-cell cache prevents same-long-street buildings from collapsing to one access node and teleporting. A transient walker entity stores its immutable path, citizen/destination generations, stable member id, purpose, and direction in `pedestrianPath`; only `{segmentIndex,t}` changes each tick in `pedestrian`, avoiding full-path and profile/history recorder diffs. A restored legacy path without a member projects the household's valid current traveller or deterministic primary-member fallback. Speed is 0.08 cells/tick, and same-directed walkers process leader-first with a no-pass clamp that preserves a 0.25-cell personal-space gap. The renderer receives only the current segment and submits exactly seven non-shadow instanced batches: top, lower garment, head, two legs, and two arms. Stable `(citizen id, generation, member id)` hashing chooses from one combined wardrobe top palette, independent lower-garment colors, six skin tones, body proportions, sleeve colour, and gait (cadence, swing scale, phase offset), keeping one named person recognizable across temporary walker ids and later outings. The walk cycle advances with distance travelled rather than the clock, so a paused city freezes mid-stride and two walkers on one route stay out of lockstep. Direction-dependent curb offsets put opposing trips on separate sidewalk lanes.
- Vehicles: entity with `vehicle` component {legs: {edge, reverse}[], legIndex, t ∈ [0,1)}. Per tick, the DESIRED advance is (cellsPerTick / edgeLength) with cellsPerTick = 0.35 x max(0.25, 1 - 0.25 x bucket(currentEdge)); the micro rules then clamp it (never below the current t): same-lane headway ≥ 0.6 cells behind the post-move leader, a red `signalPhase` holds non-final legs at a stop line 0.5 cells before signaled (≥3-approach) junctions, a blocked next lane holds the car at the edge boundary, and spawns defer while the curb entry stretch is occupied (see `simulation-realism.md`). Engine position = integer cell under current travel position (for spatial queries); renderer reads the component for smooth interpolation and offsets each car onto the right-hand lane of its travel direction. On edge change, update per-edge vehicle counts. Arrival: vehicle despawns (final-leg arrivals ignore signals — the car leaves the road), citizen waits 64–128 ticks at work, then returns; after reaching home it enters the shopping stage after a 256–512-tick cooldown.
- Topology changes vs in-flight agents: edge identity is a geometry key (string of the two endpoint cells + the first path cell). On graph rebuild the sim remaps vehicle edge ids; vehicles referencing vanished edges despawn. A pedestrian whose remaining exact-cell path contains a removed road also despawns and returns to its logical origin phase. Both cancellations increment `disconnectedTrips`; a cancelled shopper never creates a sale.
- Congestion epoch system (interval 64, offset 12; phase 3): recompute buckets for all edges; if any bucket changed, congestionEpoch++ (new paths see new costs; existing vehicles keep their route).

## Fields (civ-engine Layer<number>)

| Field | blockSize | cadence | rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| pollution | 2 | interval 8, offset 0 | decay x0.88, then each non-abandoned industrial building adds 6 x level at its block with radial falloff (radius 3 blocks, linear); coal plant adds 30. Clamp 0..100. |
| noise | 2 | interval 8, offset 6 | decay x0.85; road cells emit 1 + 2 x congestionBucket; non-abandoned commercial adds 4 x level. Clamp 0..100. |
| landValue | 4 | interval 16, offset 12 | value = 30 + 15 x nearWater(≤6 cells) + 8 x serviceCoverageCount + 4 x nearTrees(≤3) - 0.4 x pollution - 0.25 x noise, clamp 0..100. nearTrees reads the derived tree view (initial mask minus claimed footprints; see Map). |
| coverage x6 | 4 | rebuilt on service build/demolish | One binary layer per service. `coverageCountAt` groups park and garden as one green-space need, so the six layers produce at most five desirability/happiness credits. |

Layer states are mirrored into components on a dedicated singleton "mirror" entity — one component per layer, written only on that layer's recompute cadence — so save/load and replay stay correct. Components rather than world state because world-state values are JSON-fingerprinted twice per tick by the engine, while component diffs are dirty-flag-only. OccupancyGrid and other derived maps are NOT mirrored; `rebuildDerived` reconstructs them from entities.

## Services (player-placed, 2x2 footprint, must touch a road)

| Service | Cost | Upkeep/interval | Radius (cells) | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Fire station | 400 | 8 | 24 | coverage → desirability + land value |
| Police station | 400 | 8 | 24 | same |
| Clinic | 500 | 10 | 32 | same |
| School | 500 | 10 | 32 | same + gates building level 3 |
| Park | 150 | 3 | 10 | Green-space coverage and a larger leisure destination within 32 road cells. Youthful households prefer it and the youngest member represents the trip. A park is the one service that does NOT clear the trees under it. |
| Community garden | 90 | 2 | 6 | Cheaper hyperlocal green infrastructure and a quieter leisure destination within 16 road cells. Households without children/teens prefer it; a senior represents the visit when present, otherwise the oldest adult. Cultivated beds clear wild trees under the footprint. Its nominal covered area per dollar is lower than a park's, so it fills local gaps rather than replacing parks. |

Park and garden maintain separate layers, overlays, models, and outing identities, but they satisfy one green-space civic need: overlapping either kind never grants more than one desirability, land-value, or happiness credit at a home. Same-kind overlap is likewise binary.

Placing a service, power plant, wind turbine, or water pump automatically bulldozes every intersecting growable R/C/I building in full, including resident eviction and worker unassignment, then claims the special building's footprint. Roads, water, and another service/plant/pump still block placement. Underlying zoning remains unchanged, matching manual building bulldozing, while the demolished growable's whole footprint receives the normal rubble cooldown. Power lines and pipes are independent non-occupying overlays, so they neither block the special building nor get removed when it takes the growable's place.

No fire/crime/health incidents in v1 (documented later-phase candidates).

## Utilities

- Power: coal plant (800, upkeep 16, capacity 400 units, pollution source, 3x3 footprint) and wind turbine (300, upkeep 6, capacity 40, 1x1). Power line cost 4/cell; a line is a thin overhead overlay that never occupies a cell or dezones, so it runs over roads, buildings, and zoned land (a building grows freely under the wire). Unlike an underground pipe, water rejects a power line. It conducts via its own `powerLineCells` map, never `occupiedCells`, so bulldoze and save/load stay consistent with no ownership transfer. Rendered as sparse poles (line ends, corners, junctions, and one every `POLE_SPACING` cells along a straight run) with an overhead wire strung between them, so a line spans a long distance without a pole on every cell. Conduction network = plant footprints + power line cells, joined by 4-directional adjacency. ONLY the infrastructure the player places carries supply: buildings and service structures draw from the network but never relay it onward, so a plant lights only what its own reach covers and a city has to be wired street by street (a line laid across a gap is an orphan with no source). A building is connected if its footprint is within Chebyshev 5 of a network cell; a footprint straddling two separate networks draws from the first in scan order rather than merging them. Recomputed by flood-fill (interval 8, offset 7; phase 5). Demand: 1 unit x level x footprintCells per building; if total connected demand exceeds capacity, buildings are powered in ascending entity-id order until budget exhausts (deterministic brownout).
- Water: pump (500, upkeep 10, capacity 300 units, must be orthogonally adjacent to water) and pipes (3/new cell, placeable across land or water and under roads/buildings as a separate non-occupying sub-grid overlay). Every new lake cell has the same $3 cost as land; overlapping existing pipe cells are free. Pipes are underground and invisible during normal play; their near-ground planning hints remain visible over lake cells while the Water overlay is active. Building connected if footprint within Chebyshev 5 of a pipe network reachable from a pump. Flood-fill (interval 8, offset 1; phase 5). Demand 1 x level x cells. No separate sewage in v1.
- Unpowered or unwatered buildings cannot level up and accumulate abandonment (see Zoning; missing power/water abandons ONLY on the longer 75-evaluation ≈60s utility grace, never the fast 8s score path). While such a building is still LIVE it floats a gently-bobbing problem icon (⚡ no power, 💧 no water, or both) above its roof — an always-on, no-overlay-needed "fix me before I abandon" warning like the reference game. The icon clears the moment the building is served or abandons (a dead grey building shows nothing; the advisor's dark-city guidance covers that case). Rendered client-side as compact amber-diamond/blue-circle vector billboards (`UtilityIconsFx`) rather than font glyphs, reconciled per building upsert from the `powered`/`watered` flags and submitted in at most three instanced batches (power, water, both).

## Economy

- Treasury starts at 20,000. Each budget interval (1024 ticks; budget system interval 1024, offset 1023): base income = Σ buildings taxRate(zone)/100 x taxBase x level x footprintCells, taxBase R 20, C 30, I 30 (abandoned pay nothing); retail income = completed pending visits x 25 spend/visit x commercialTaxRate/100. A retail visit enters the pending counter only when the shopper physically reaches the still-valid commercial destination, and the budget consumes that counter exactly once while preserving the lifetime completed-shopping count. Expenses = Σ upkeep (services, utilities) + ROAD_UPKEEP_PER_CELL x land road cells + BRIDGE_UPKEEP_PER_CELL x bridge cells, where ROAD_UPKEEP_PER_CELL = 0.1 and BRIDGE_UPKEEP_PER_CELL = 0.4 per budget interval. Tax sliders per zone 0–20%, default 9%.
- Placement validators reject any purchase the treasury cannot cover. Treasury may go negative from upkeep; while negative, all purchases are blocked EXCEPT power and water items (plants, lines, pumps, pipes) — a broke, unpowered city otherwise has no income path and would soft-lock unrecoverably — and a "city is broke" warning shows.

## UI surface (v1)

Toolbar: select/inspect, road, bulldoze, zone R/C/I, dezone, services x6, coal/wind, power line, pump, pipe. Each tool has a single-key shortcut shown as a badge on its button and wired globally (ignored while a modifier is held or a text field is focused): W/A/S/D are reserved for camera panning, so the tools avoid them — R/C/I select the zones (the classic mnemonic), Road Q, Select V, Bulldoze B, Dezone X, Fire F, Police P, Clinic H, School E, Park N, Garden M, Coal G, Wind K, Line L, Pump U, Pipe J. Camera: left-drag pans (with the Select tool), scroll-wheel zooms, right-drag or middle(wheel)-drag orbits, and W/A/S/D pan the focus across the terrain surface screen-relative (speed scales with zoom distance; clamped to the map). Space toggles pause ⇄ resume (resuming to the last non-zero speed) — the standard game convention; it's guarded against text fields and preventDefaults the page scroll. Top HUD: treasury (+ per-interval delta), population, live vehicle and pedestrian counts, power/water capacity meters, RCI demand bars, day counter, speed controls, and a Budget button toggling a top-right panel with the last interval's income/expenses/net, separately identified retail tax, and per-zone tax sliders (0–20%, authoritative rates echoed back from sim frame stats). The HUD, advisor, budget, inspect, legend, toast, milestone, and shortcut-badge chrome share a friendly light frosted-aqua planning skin (`src/ui/hud-style.ts`) with dark readable text, cheerful cyan active tool states, and green/orange city-status accents, so the UI feels inviting without losing contrast over the map. The ⚡/💧 meters read `load/capacity` (total building demand vs installed plant/pump capacity from the frame stats; demand counts every building, abandoned included, so it never blanks when a city goes dark) and turn warm when the network is over capacity — telling a player whether an unpowered/unwatered city needs another plant/pump (over capacity) or just more lines/pipes (capacity covers the load). Hidden until the city has buildings drawing or a source installed. Overlays menu: pollution, noise, land value, traffic (edge congestion coloring), power and water (network cells, 5-cell connection reach halo, unsupplied buildings in red), and one coverage overlay per service — fire, police, health, education, parks, and gardens. While any overlay is active the world (terrain, water, buildings, roads, sky, fog) renders in greyscale and the overlay itself is the only colored thing on screen — informational surfaces (the overlay planes, build ghost, indicators, problem icons) keep their color, overlay materials ignore distance fog, and switching back to None restores full color instantly. While an overlay is active a colour-key legend (bottom-left) explains its scale — a low→high gradient for the field overlays (e.g. Clean→Heavy, Low→High) and labelled swatches for traffic (Flowing→Jammed) and power/water (network / supplied / no-supply); it hides when no overlay is active. In Select mode, pedestrians get a pointer cursor and hover ring, a forgiving person-height pick target whose overlapping hits prefer the walker center nearest the pointer, solid-building/service/utility occlusion, a 100 ms stationary-pointer refresh as people or the camera move, and a persistent selected ring when their current walker is present. Clicking one opens a HUD-safe scrollable named-resident panel, refreshed on demand no more often than every 500 ms of wall-clock time without replacing its action button or keyboard focus, with the selected person's age/life stage/education/role, all three household members, truthful active-traveller versus idle activity-representative wording, household happiness and reasons, home/work/activity venue/destination/commute, biography completeness/truncation provenance, and newest life events. Cyan and orange map anchors show home and work; the generation-checked building-or-service `destinationPlace` drives magenta while travelling, including park/garden trips and the return home, while `activityPlace` remains venue provenance through the return leg and drives magenta only while dwelling. Click an occupied residential building and choose “Meet a resident” to cycle its people with a full citizen id/generation/member cursor and without streaming resident ids; its displayed total reconciles against live home occupancy. `render_game_to_text()` exposes the same bounded generation-guarded inspection evidence. Building clicks still show zone, level, footprint, residents/jobs, and ⚡/💧 connection status; a selected service shows its coverage radius. Toasts appear for rejected commands, stale inspection identities, and save/load results; a HUD warning badge shows broke and disconnected-trips states.

Advisor panel (bottom right): a scrollable, prioritized guidance list. Two row kinds. **Tips** are guided checklists — the onboarding/utility steps (found the city by linking a road to the highway; zone R/C/I then grow a building; place a plant then power every building; place a pump then water every building). The power and water tips appear from ZONING time (as soon as any zone is painted), not just once buildings exist, so a player is prompted to pre-wire before the first buildings start their utility-grace clock — the power/water tip then stays until every built cell is actually served. Once a plant/pump exists but buildings stay dark/dry, the tip's second step names the real bottleneck from the frame stats' supply-vs-demand signal: if the network is over capacity it says "add another plant/pump" (more lines won't help); otherwise it says "drag Lines/Pipes" (capacity is fine, the buildings just aren't connected). Each step shows a green ✓ when its live condition is met (an ○ while pending); a tip has no dismiss button and stays expanded until EVERY step is satisfied, then drops out on its own — so a tip only collapses once all its requirements are done. While any tip still has an unmet step the whole panel is pinned open and the header collapse control is hidden, so guidance can't be tucked away early; once no tip is pending the panel collapses normally. Until the city is linked to the highway, only the founding tip shows. **Plain advisories** (dismissable, return if the situation persists; clickable ones fly the camera to the problem and flash it): abandonment, unroutable commuters, budget deficit (last interval's expenses exceeded income while solvent), demand pressure, unemployment, and the broke state.

## Save/load

`world.serialize()` (layer states already mirrored into the singleton mirror entity's components; cold citizen profiles and life events are ordinary serialized components; OccupancyGrid and other derived maps are NOT mirrored — `rebuildDerived` reconstructs them from entities) + game meta {saveVersion: 1, seed}. One localStorage slot; Load applies on a fresh page boot. Load = fresh world via world-factory, `applySnapshot`, `rebuildDerived` (road graph from road cells, layers from mirror components, occupancy from entity footprints). Pre-profile saves remain version 1: missing citizen profile/history components are read through deterministic fallback identity whose `legacyFallback` provenance and unavailable prior history are explicit, while an in-flight pedestrian path missing its member uses a deterministic selectable fallback.

## Definition of "fully functioning" (v1 acceptance)

From an empty map a player (or the automated playtest) can: build a road network, zone, place power+water, and reach ≥ 1,000 population with visibly moving traffic; congestion emerges when overloading one artery and re-routes after building an alternative; overlays reflect sim fields; taxes/upkeep produce a live budget that can go broke and recover; save → reload reproduces the city; determinism self-check passes; performance acceptance for a 128x128 city at target scale is nominal 60 Hz presentation / 20 TPS simulation on this dev machine. Because a nominal 60 Hz browser does not deliver every callback at exactly 16.667 ms, the reproducible gate requires mean ≥58 fps, p95 ≤18.5 ms, p99 ≤25 ms, no three consecutive >20 ms frames, and active simulation throughput ≥18 TPS at 1× and ≥72 TPS at 4×; it also pins the exact render buffer and requires a stationary paused tick and zero browser errors.
